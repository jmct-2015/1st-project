Valve launches the Steam Machine

The Steam Machine project, initially launched and subsequently discontinued in the late 2010s, has been revived by Valve with a new console announced in November 2025. This second iteration is a dedicated living room gaming PC designed to provide a console-like experience for a user's entire Steam library. The original machines failed due to a lack of compatible native Linux games at the time, a problem the new version aims to solve with the mature Proton compatibility layer from the Steam Deck.

Key Information:

Release Date: Expected to launch in early 2026, with pricing yet to be announced, but aimed to be competitive with a mid-range gaming PC.

Form Factor: A compact, cube-shaped device (approx. 6.3 inches per side) with a built-in power supply and an LED light strip on the front for status updates.

Operating System: Runs on SteamOS, Valve's Linux-based, gaming-optimized operating system, ensuring a seamless interface familiar to Steam Deck users.

Performance: Touted as over six times more powerful than the Steam Deck, capable of 4K gaming at 60 frames per second using FSR upscaling, and powerful enough for demanding AAA titles.

Hardware Specs: Features a semi-custom AMD Zen 4 6-core/12-thread CPU, a semi-custom AMD RDNA 3 GPU with 28 CUs and 8GB GDDR6 VRAM, and 16GB of DDR5 RAM.

Storage: Available in 512GB SSD and 2TB SSD options, both with expandable storage via a microSD card slot.

Connectivity: Includes multiple ports (DisplayPort 1.4, HDMI 2.0, one USB-C, and four USB-A ports), Wi-Fi 6E, and Bluetooth 5.3.

New Controller: Will launch alongside a newly redesigned Steam Controller, featuring dual trackpads, back buttons, and low-latency custom wireless connectivity.

Ecosystem: Part of a new hardware lineup including a "Steam Frame" VR headset, all running the same SteamOS to create a unified PC gaming ecosystem in the living room.

Game Compatibility: A "Steam Machine Verified" program will rate game compatibility, similar to the Steam Deck, ensuring users know how games will perform.
